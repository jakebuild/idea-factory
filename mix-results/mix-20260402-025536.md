### Idea 1: PoseProof

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved users will pay for a blunt attractiveness score reveal plus a concrete improvement plan

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - Share

**Concept:** Record a 5-second slow-turn selfie video instead of guessing with 20 nearly identical photos. The app auto-picks your highest-scoring frame for dating apps or Instagram, grades angle, smile, eye openness, and posture, then gives one precise retake instruction to beat your current best. The loop is shoot, get the keeper frame, reshoot, and watch your score improve.

**Reveal moment:** It freezes the exact best frame from your video and shows that this angle scores 18 points higher than the selfie you were about to post.

**The hook:** It tells you the single frame from a 5-second video where you look hottest, instead of making you guess.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is reliable frame ranking, but a vision model plus simple heuristics is enough for v1.

### Idea 2: NativeMinute

**Targets:** Intelligence

**Copies:** ELSA Speak — proved people pay for instant pronunciation feedback because sounding fluent changes how smart and competent they are perceived to be

**Blocks used:** Microphone - Voice synthesis - Streak

**Concept:** The user reads one short sentence aloud each day for interviews, meetings, or sales calls. The app gives a blunt Native Score, highlights the exact word or sound that gives them away, then plays a corrected version and lets them retry immediately to hear the difference. The loop is speak, see the weak spot, mimic, retry, and protect the streak.

**Reveal moment:** It marks one word in red, plays your version against the corrected version, and your Native Score jumps from 61 to 74 on the retry.

**The hook:** It does not teach all of English; it tells you the exact sound making you sound less credible in a meeting.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is phoneme-level feedback, but speech-to-text plus pronunciation scoring APIs can cover v1.

### Idea 3: SnoreReceipt

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** SnoreLab — proved people will pay to quantify sleep-breathing problems because hearing proof of the problem is more compelling than generic sleep advice

**Blocks used:** Microphone - Sleep - Share

**Concept:** Put the phone on the bedside table and the app records only snore events, not the whole night. In the morning it shows a blunt Snore Score, total snore minutes, the loudest 10-second clip, and a simple trend line against previous nights so users can test changes like side-sleeping, alcohol, or earlier bedtime. The loop is sleep, get the receipt, try one change, and compare tomorrow.

**Reveal moment:** You wake up to a screen that says you snored for 84 minutes and can tap the exact 10-second clip where you sounded worst.

**The hook:** People will share it because hearing your own snoring is equal parts horrifying, funny, and impossible to ignore.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is filtering speech and background noise, but bedtime audio event detection is very manageable for a first version.
