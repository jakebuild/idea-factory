### Idea 1: GlowMirror

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved people will pay for a blunt attractiveness score and a clear "fix this first" improvement path

**Blocks used:** Camera - Light sensor - Challenge

**Concept:** You take a live selfie before work, a date, or a night out, and the app gives you a "Date-Ready Score" based on lighting, posture, skin shine, eye fatigue, and framing. Instead of generic beauty tips, it gives one fast challenge like "move to window light" or "lift the camera 10 degrees," then lets you rescan and watch the score jump. The loop is simple: check, fix, rescan, save your best setup for the next time.

**Reveal moment:** "Date-Ready Score 61 - your face is fine, your overhead lighting is killing you; move two steps toward the window for an estimated +13."

**The hook:** It tells you in ten seconds whether you actually look bad or your lighting is lying to you.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — hardest part is making the scoring feel specific and credible without pretending to deliver scientific beauty truth.

### Idea 2: Leave With It

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Alarmy — proved daily pain plus forced action creates habit retention far better than gentle reminders

**Blocks used:** NFC - WiFi - Consequences

**Concept:** You stick cheap NFC tags on your wallet, keys, badge, meds, or laptop charger, and the app arms a "leave home" check when your phone disconnects from home WiFi during chosen hours. If an essential item has not been tapped that morning, the app throws a loud lock-screen alert and keeps nagging until you confirm the missing item or deliberately skip it. Different packs cover work, gym, travel, or school, so the app becomes a front-door ritual instead of a passive checklist.

**Reveal moment:** "Missing before exit: work badge and meds - tap both now or this alert keeps following you."

**The hook:** It turns forgetting your essentials into Alarmy for your front door.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — hardest part is working around background and NFC friction cleanly enough that the ritual feels protective, not annoying.

### Idea 3: VibeCheck Voice

**Targets:** Sex / Dating

**Copies:** Rizz — proved people will pay to avoid dating embarrassment when the payoff is an instant verdict on what to say next

**Blocks used:** Microphone - LLM - Challenge

**Concept:** You speak the voice note, first-date story, or opening line you are about to send, and the app scores how you come across: confident, boring, needy, try-hard, flirty, or awkward. It highlights the exact sentence that hurts your vibe, rewrites it in three tones, and gives you a quick retry challenge so you can record a stronger version immediately. The loop is rehearse, score, fix, send, then come back before the next important date or voice note.

**Reveal moment:** "Confidence 72, neediness 58 - cut the line about being 'free whenever' and your reply odds jump."

**The hook:** It tells you what your crush hears when you think you sound confident.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — hardest part is keeping the scoring sharp and consistent enough that users trust the feedback after one listen.
