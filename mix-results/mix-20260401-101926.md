### Idea 1: Voice Score

**Targets:** Attractiveness — fear of sounding unattractive, awkward, or unconfident

**Copies:** UMax — proved that people will pay to have an AI rate something they're insecure about; UMax does ~$1M+/mo on face rating alone

**Blocks used:** Microphone - AI (voice analysis) - Score reveal

**Concept:** You record 30 seconds of yourself talking. The AI scores your voice across dimensions — warmth, confidence, clarity, pace, pitch variety — and gives you a brutal breakdown. No face required. The insecurity around how your voice sounds is massively underserved: everyone has heard a recording of themselves and cringed, but no app has made a business out of rating it. Daily re-recordings let you track improvement over time.

**Reveal moment:** Your voice score (1–10) flashes on screen with a single-line verdict: "You sound hesitant — here's why people tune you out."

**The hook:** "I recorded my voice and an AI told me I sound like a nervous intern — it was uncomfortably accurate."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — microphone input + Whisper/Claude for analysis + a scored rubric. Hardest part is making the scoring feel credible enough that users trust and share it rather than dismiss it as random.

### Idea 2: Profile Roast

**Targets:** Sex / Dating — fear of being undateable, invisible, or skipped past

**Copies:** UMax — proved that people pay to know why they're failing at attractiveness; dating profile version extends the same visceral reveal moment into the $3B dating app economy

**Blocks used:** Camera (photo upload) - LLM - Score reveal

**Concept:** You upload your dating profile bio + 1–3 photos. The AI roasts your profile like a brutally honest friend: scores it 1–10, explains exactly why you're getting ghosted, then rewrites your bio for free. Unlock the rewritten photos (optimized pose/crop suggestions) with a one-time $3.99 purchase. The roast is shareable — a card with your score and the single most brutal line — making the distribution loop self-funding.

**Reveal moment:** Your profile score appears (e.g. "5/10 — Your first photo looks like a LinkedIn headshot. Nobody swipes right on LinkedIn.") with a swipe-up to see the full breakdown.

**The hook:** "My dating profile scored 5/10 and the AI told me my bio 'reads like a terms of service agreement' — so I fixed it."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — photo upload + Claude API for scoring and rewrite + simple shareable card generation. Hardest part is prompting the AI to be consistently brutal-but-accurate rather than generically mean; calibration takes iteration.

### Idea 3: Argue This

**Targets:** Intelligence — fear of being wrong, outthought, or unable to hold your own in an argument

**Copies:** Wordle — proved that a single daily challenge + shareable score card drives massive organic distribution with zero content spend; Duolingo proved that streak + score beats motivation every time

**Blocks used:** LLM (AI debate opponent) - Daily challenge mechanic - Share (score card)

**Concept:** Every day at midnight, a new debate topic drops (e.g. "Remote work makes people less creative"). You pick a side, make your argument in 3 minutes via text or voice, and an AI argues back — hard. After 5 exchanges, your argument is scored 1–10 across logic, evidence, and clarity. One shot per day, no retries. Share your score card the same way you share your Wordle result. Weekly streak = leaderboard rank.

**Reveal moment:** Your argument score flashes with the AI's verdict: "7/10 — Strong opening, but you conceded the strongest counterpoint without realizing it."

**The hook:** "I argued with an AI for 5 minutes and got a 6/10 — my friend got a 9 on the same topic and won't stop telling me about it."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — daily topic curation (start with a CSV of 90 pre-written topics), Claude API for debate and scoring, shareable card image. Hardest part is making the AI feel like a genuinely challenging opponent rather than a pushover or a straw man.
