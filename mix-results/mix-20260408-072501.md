### Idea 1: QuietDot

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Locket Widget — reached profitability in 2024, proving a glanceable home-screen relationship ritual can become a daily habit people keep

**Blocks used:** Home screen widget - One-tap check-in - Escalation timer

**Concept:** QuietDot is for adult children who are low-grade anxious about an aging parent living alone. The parent taps one giant "I'm okay" button each morning, and the child sees that status directly on a home-screen widget; if the check-in is missed, the widget shifts from calm green to amber, then escalates into a call-now state with a backup contact flow. It copies Locket's emotional-widget habit, but swaps "send me a photo" for "show me they're okay."

**Reveal moment:** Your widget flips from green "Mom checked in at 7:14 AM" to amber "No check-in yet today," and you feel the problem before you even open the app.

**The hook:** It replaces the guilty daily "just checking on you" text with a one-glance answer.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — widget, simple paired accounts, push notifications, and escalation rules are feasible in 2-4 weeks; the hardest part is making the parent onboarding flow frictionless enough that non-technical users actually complete it.

### Idea 2: SocialBattery

**Targets:** Social Status / Fear of Embarrassment

**Copies:** WHOOP — grew to 2.5M members and over $1B in 2025 bookings, proving people will organize their day around a single readiness score

**Blocks used:** Calendar input - HealthKit sleep data - Daily score reveal

**Concept:** SocialBattery predicts whether you have enough real human energy for the version of yourself your calendar demands today. It combines last night's sleep, recent meeting load, and today's schedule into a 0-100 Social Battery score, then flags the exact event most likely to get your awkward, flat, or irritated version and suggests one recovery move before that happens. It is WHOOP for people scared of showing up socially depleted rather than physically under-recovered.

**Reveal moment:** A morning card says, "Battery 41%: you will probably crash before the 7:30 PM dinner unless you take a 22-minute reset at 5:10 PM."

**The hook:** It tells you which event is about to get the worst version of you before you embarrass yourself there.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — calendar sync, HealthKit reads, and a rule-based scoring model are all solo-dev realistic; the hardest part is making the score feel uncannily accurate within the first few days so users trust it.

### Idea 3: LicenseCliff

**Targets:** Fear of Loss / Fear of Losing Income

**Copies:** NEXT Insurance — generated $548M in 2024 revenue, proving workers and small business owners pay quickly when paperwork gaps threaten their livelihood

**Blocks used:** OCR document scan - Expiration countdown - Wallet/PDF export

**Concept:** LicenseCliff is for cosmetologists, bartenders, nurses, real-estate agents, and contractors who keep their income alive through scattered credentials. You scan a license, certification, or permit once; the app extracts expiry dates, renewal steps, continuing-education requirements, and supporting documents, then turns that into a blunt income-risk dashboard showing what is protected and what is about to break. It copies NEXT's "paperwork reassurance" model, but compresses it into a solo-friendly consumer tool built around personal compliance cliffs.

**Reveal moment:** The app opens to a red card that says, "Your food handler card expires in 11 days, and two scheduled shifts next month require it."

**The hook:** It turns boring renewals into an explicit countdown to lost shifts and lost money.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — OCR, deadline tracking, reminders, and exportable renewal checklists are straightforward; the hardest part is handling messy document formats across states and professions without making the parser brittle.
