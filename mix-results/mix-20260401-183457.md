### Idea 1: PuffScore

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved people will pay for a blunt attractiveness score and a personalized "fix this first" upsell

**Blocks used:** Camera - Sleep - Object recognition

**Concept:** Scan your dinner, dessert, or drink order and log your planned sleep time, and the app predicts how rough your face will look tomorrow morning: puffiness, acne risk, dullness, and "camera-ready by" time. The loop is fast and ugly in the right way: scan tonight, see tomorrow's damage, then follow one simple save-it action like extra water, earlier bedtime, or skipping the salty add-on. It borrows the numeric reveal psychology from face-rating apps, but points it at the part people actually worry about at night: "what will I look like tomorrow?"

**Reveal moment:** Your ramen and two drinks turn into a 7.8 Puff Score with "visible under-eye swelling by 8:00 AM" on one screen.

**The hook:** It tells you tonight whether tomorrow's face will betray you.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is making the scoring feel believable with a narrow v1 heuristic instead of pretending to be medically precise.

### Idea 2: DoseLock

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Alarmy — proved forced action plus visible consequences creates sticky daily behavior and paid conversion

**Blocks used:** NFC - Consequences - Recovery

**Concept:** Put an NFC sticker on a pill bottle, inhaler, or supplement pack, set a medication window, and the app refuses to mark you safe until you tap the real bottle and confirm the dose. If you miss it, your protection streak visibly cracks, the app escalates into an ugly red "unprotected today" state, and recovery takes several clean days, which makes adherence feel like something you can lose, not just a reminder you can swipe away. It is Alarmy for the people who already know reminders do not work on them.

**Reveal moment:** One bottle tap flips today's shield from red to green; miss the window and your protection streak fractures in front of you.

**The hook:** Alarmy for meds is harder to ignore than another polite notification.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is reliable reminder timing and a clean NFC onboarding flow across iPhone and Android.

### Idea 3: FirstFit

**Targets:** Sex / Dating

**Copies:** Tinder — proved people obsess over tiny first-impression gains when the payoff is more matches and more dates

**Blocks used:** Camera - Weather - Share

**Concept:** Take three mirror selfies before a date, night out, or post, and the app ranks which outfit gives you the strongest right-swipe energy for that context. It scores fit, color contrast, grooming coherence, and weather appropriateness, then gives one blunt fix like "keep the jacket, lose the chain" so the loop is snap, rank, change, rescan. The value is not fashion inspiration; it is ending uncertainty right before the moment that matters.

**Reveal moment:** Three mirror selfies go in, and one comes back stamped "highest match-rate look for tonight" with the exact item dragging the others down.

**The hook:** It ends the 20-minute "which outfit is hottest?" spiral before you leave the house.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is keeping the ranking explanations specific enough that users trust the result instead of treating it like random AI taste.
