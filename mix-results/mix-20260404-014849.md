### Idea 1: HealthWrapped

**Targets:** Status / Social Pride

**Copies:** Spotify Wrapped — proved annual/monthly data reveals go massively viral with zero marketing spend; Spotify reportedly acquired 30M+ new users from a single Wrapped campaign

**Blocks used:** Apple HealthKit data - beautiful card generation - social share mechanic

**Concept:** Once a month, HealthWrapped pulls all your Apple Health data and generates a Spotify Wrapped-style animated card with your top stats: highest single-day step count, worst sleep week, exact hour your heart rate peaks, and one surprising pattern the algorithm found. The card is designed to be screenshot and posted — not to feel like a health app, but to feel like a personality reveal.

**Reveal moment:** A full-screen animated card drops: "Your March — 87 miles walked, heart rate spiked every Sunday at 9 PM, and you improved sleep consistency by 18% from February" — tap to share.

**The hook:** People screenshot it and post it the way they post Spotify Wrapped — it makes your health data feel like a flex, not a chore.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — HealthKit API is well-documented on iOS, card generation is pure front-end UI work, no ML required; hardest part is making the card visually compelling enough to actually get shared.

### Idea 2: FocusDebt

**Targets:** Productivity Anxiety / Fear of Wasted Time

**Copies:** Robinhood — proved that wrapping abstract losses in financial/portfolio metaphors makes them feel concrete and urgent enough to obsessively check; Robinhood hit $1B revenue by making investing feel like a leaderboard

**Blocks used:** Screen Time API - financial portfolio dashboard UI - debt framing mechanic

**Concept:** FocusDebt applies the Robinhood portfolio dashboard to your Screen Time data. Each app category is an "investment" of your attention — deep work goes up, scrolling goes down. Your weekly "focus portfolio" is either up or down, with a running distraction debt shown in hours and estimated productivity value. The metaphor reframes time waste in financial terms your brain is already trained to feel bad about.

**Reveal moment:** The app opens to a red screen: "Your attention portfolio is down 31% this week. Distraction debt: 4.2 hours. Estimated productivity cost: $340."

**The hook:** "My focus portfolio is down 31% this week" is a completely different feeling than "I was on my phone too much" — the financial framing makes it viscerally shareable.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — Screen Time API available on iOS with permission, dashboard UI is chart-based and well-trodden; hardest part is calibrating the "productivity value" formula in a way that feels credible and not arbitrary.

### Idea 3: SleepFace

**Targets:** Vanity / Health Anxiety

**Copies:** FaceApp — proved viral transformation mechanics make people obsessively return and share results; FaceApp reportedly did $80M+ in lifetime revenue from a simple before/after reveal

**Blocks used:** Front camera - AI face analysis - Apple HealthKit sleep data

**Concept:** SleepFace asks you to take a quick morning selfie daily. It uses AI to score your face for visible sleep debt — puffiness, eye bags, redness, skin tone — and maps it against your actual sleep hours from HealthKit. Over time it builds your personal "sleep face fingerprint," showing you side-by-side how your face changes with one good week of sleep vs. your current state after a run of bad nights.

**Reveal moment:** Side-by-side: your face after your best sleep week vs. your face today, with a blunt delta — "Your face is showing 3.2 days of sleep debt right now."

**The hook:** "The app showed me my own face after a good week vs. now and I actually started going to bed on time."

**Solo ship check:** Moderate — front camera + face analysis is achievable with a Vision API (Google Cloud Vision or AWS Rekognition handles face attribute detection), but building a "personal baseline" model that feels accurate requires consistent daily use to feel meaningful; MVP works with relative scoring on puffiness and redness without custom ML.
