### Idea 1: MirrorMargin

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved people will pay for a blunt attractiveness verdict and a clear "fix this first" upgrade path

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - Share

**Concept:** You take 3 mirror selfies before a date, party, or interview, and the app ranks your outfits by attractiveness, polish, and "looks expensive" impression. It highlights exactly what drags the weak outfit down, like bad shoe choice, washed-out color contrast, or messy layering, then tells you the single swap most likely to lift the score. It copies UMax's harsh verdict loop but moves it from face anxiety to full-body style anxiety.

**Reveal moment:** Three outfit photos appear side by side and one gets stamped `Best First Impression` with a blunt score gap over the others.

**The hook:** It ends the "which outfit actually makes me look hotter?" group-chat debate in ten seconds.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is making outfit scoring feel consistent, so v1 should stay narrow: mirror photos, a few occasions, and one concrete fix.

### Idea 2: Hangover Receipt

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Cal AI — proved people will repeatedly scan what they consume if the payoff is an instant scary number reveal; reportedly does $3M+/mo

**Blocks used:** Camera - Sleep - Object recognition

**Concept:** You scan a beer, cocktail, wine list, or liquor shelf, enter your planned bedtime, and the app predicts tomorrow's hangover severity, sleep disruption, and earliest "functional again" time. The loop is scan before you order, see the damage, then swap to a safer option, pace yourself, or accept the pain. It keeps Cal AI's consumption-scan mechanic but sells fear of tomorrow instead of calories.

**Reveal moment:** The app shows `Tomorrow Penalty: 74/100` with a projected wake-up state and the exact hour you'll likely feel normal again.

**The hook:** It tells you whether one more drink is stealing tomorrow before you pay for it.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is a believable scoring model, but v1 can ship with rule-based estimates from drink type, size, bedtime, and recent sleep.

### Idea 3: DoseLock

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Alarmy — proved that consequences beat motivation and forced action creates a daily retention loop

**Blocks used:** NFC - Consequences - Streak

**Concept:** You stick a cheap NFC tag on your pill bottle, inhaler, or supplement jar, and the reminder cannot be dismissed until you physically tap the item. Miss the tap and the app escalates with louder alarms, a broken protection streak, and a lock-screen warning that makes the loss feel immediate instead of abstract. It is Alarmy for people who are scared of forgetting the thing that keeps them stable.

**Reveal moment:** The moment you tap the bottle, the screen flips from red countdown to a green `Dose Protected` shield for today.

**The hook:** It turns medication adherence from a forgettable notification into a physical ritual you feel relieved to complete.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is reliable reminder and NFC onboarding on iPhone, but the core v1 is still a local-only reminders app with tag validation.
