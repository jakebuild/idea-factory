### Idea 1: BufferScore

**Targets:** Fear of Loss / Financial Security

**Copies:** Credit Karma — 130M users and a $1.8B acquisition proved people obsessively check a free anxiety-inducing score when it tells them how exposed they are

**Blocks used:** score reveal - benchmark percentiles - weekly drift alerts

**Concept:** BufferScore is for salaried workers who vaguely fear layoffs but have no number for how screwed they would be if it happened next month. You enter your savings, fixed expenses, role, industry, and rough interview readiness, and the app turns that into a Layoff Buffer Score that combines runway, hiring climate, and bounce-back odds. What makes it unique is that it does not just track savings, it turns job insecurity into one score that gets better or worse every week.

**Reveal moment:** The app lands on, "Layoff Buffer Score: 34/100 — at your current burn, one bad month leaves you with 7.4 weeks of runway and below-median rehire odds."

**The hook:** It gives white-collar workers a Credit Karma-style number for how panicked they should be about losing their paycheck.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the MVP is a scoring engine, benchmarks, and reminders; the hardest part is making the score feel sharp and credible without pretending to predict layoffs.

### Idea 2: PuppyWindow

**Targets:** Fear of Embarrassment / Fear of Raising a Bad Dog

**Copies:** Wonder Weeks — 5M+ downloads and a paid install proved caregivers obsessively track narrow developmental windows when they fear messing up something time-sensitive

**Blocks used:** age timeline - weekly tasks - readiness score

**Concept:** PuppyWindow is for first-time dog owners who are scared they are missing the short windows that shape whether their dog becomes calm, social, and safe in public. You enter breed, age, and a few behavior notes, and the app gives you this week's socialization, bite-inhibition, crate, and leash priorities with a simple Training Window Score. What makes it unique is that it does not offer generic dog tips, it tells you exactly which developmental window is open right now and what happens if you waste it.

**Reveal moment:** The app flashes, "Your puppy's stranger-socialization window closes in 16 days and you've completed only 4 of the 12 exposures recommended for this stage."

**The hook:** It turns vague puppy-owner guilt into a brutal weekly countdown tied to whether your dog will embarrass you for years.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + age-based timelines, task tracking, and rules-based coaching are feasible for one developer; the hardest part is narrowing the breed and training guidance enough that v1 feels trustworthy.

### Idea 3: Migraine Window

**Targets:** Health / Fear of Losing the Day

**Copies:** Remy AI: Sleep & Health Coach — 141 reviews proved users will pay for a physiology-driven timeline that explains why today will feel bad and what to change now

**Blocks used:** Apple Health sleep import - weather trigger data - risk timeline

**Concept:** Migraine Window is for people who keep having a whole day wrecked by migraines but only understand the trigger after it is too late. The app combines sleep, caffeine, cycle, stress, and local pressure changes into a next-24-hours risk timeline, then gives one preventive action like hydration timing, caffeine cutoff, or schedule protection. What makes it unique is that it is not a symptom diary first, it is a "when is today likely to go sideways?" app.

**Reveal moment:** A timeline card says, "Your highest migraine-risk window starts at 3:10 PM today because you slept 5h 12m and barometric pressure is dropping fast."

**The hook:** It tells migraine-prone people when today is about to disappear before the headache starts.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + sleep import, trigger logging, weather APIs, and rules-based timelines are realistic in 2-4 weeks; the hardest part is being useful without making medical claims the product cannot support.
