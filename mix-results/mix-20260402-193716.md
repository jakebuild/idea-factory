### Idea 1: Morning Face Receipt

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved blunt appearance scores and a single "fix this first" upsell convert because people want certainty fast

**Blocks used:** Camera - Light sensor - Streak

**Concept:** You take the same guided 5-second selfie each morning, and the app scores tiredness, under-eye shadow, skin redness, and oiliness against your own best baseline instead of a fake universal beauty standard. Then it gives one fast fix like "wait 12 minutes before your photo," "move to window light," or "blot forehead first," and lets you rescan to watch the score recover. It is a better version of face-rating apps because the product is not "are you attractive?" but "do you look worse than your normal today?"

**Reveal moment:** "Morning Face Score 61 - you look 24% more tired than your best weekday baseline, mostly from under-eye shadow."

**The hook:** It tells you whether you actually look rough today or whether you are just spiraling before a date, meeting, or post.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is enforcing consistent capture so the score reflects face changes instead of lighting noise.

### Idea 2: Snore Receipt

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** SnoreLab — proved people will pay to quantify a sleep-breathing problem when the app gives them proof they can hear and compare

**Blocks used:** Microphone - Gyroscope - Challenge

**Concept:** You leave your phone on the bed or nightstand, and the app logs only snore events while also inferring whether you were on your back or side when they happened. In the morning it shows your Snore Minutes, your worst 10-second clip, and the percentage of your snoring that happened in each sleep position, then gives you one 3-night experiment like pillow elevation or side-sleeping. It is a better version of SnoreLab because it does not just prove that you snore; it points to the simplest thing to test tonight.

**Reveal moment:** "You snored for 43 minutes last night, and 81% of it happened while you were on your back."

**The hook:** It turns vague sleep shame into one brutal receipt and one obvious experiment instead of generic sleep advice.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is making position detection feel reliable enough with simple motion heuristics and honest confidence labels.

### Idea 3: Anxiety Window

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Remy AI — proved people will pay for personalized coaching that explains today's likely problem instead of just logging yesterday's data

**Blocks used:** Sleep - Calendar - LLM

**Concept:** Each morning the app reads last night's sleep and today's calendar, then predicts the 1-2 hour window when you are most likely to feel fried, irritable, or mentally slow. It shows one reason in plain English like "short sleep + back-to-back meetings + no lunch gap," then gives one pre-emptive move such as a 15-minute walk block, a caffeine cutoff, or a rescheduled deep-work slot, and asks that evening whether the forecast was right. It is better than mood trackers because it tries to prevent the crash before it happens.

**Reveal moment:** "High-risk window today: 2:40 PM to 4:10 PM - you slept 5h 18m and stacked three meetings with no recovery gap."

**The hook:** It feels like the app saw your bad afternoon coming before you did.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is keeping the forecast simple and credible with heuristics before adding any fancy personalization.
