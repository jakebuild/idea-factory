### Idea 1: LiftGrade

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Cal AI — proved people will repeatedly scan what they put their body through if the payoff is an instant blunt score reveal; Cal AI reportedly does $3M+/mo with photo-based analysis

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - Challenge

**Concept:** Record a 5-10 second set of squats, push-ups, lunges, or deadlifts, and the app scores injury risk, depth, symmetry, and whether you are actually loading the target muscle group. The loop is simple: film one set, get a Form Damage Score, fix one cue, and retest immediately. It is Cal AI for workout anxiety instead of calorie anxiety, with a tighter payoff than a generic fitness tracker.

**Reveal moment:** Your rep freezes with red overlays on the bad positions and a blunt card that says something like "7/10 injury risk, knees collapsing, glutes barely loading."

**The hook:** It tells you whether your "healthy" workout is actually wasting reps and beating up your joints.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is making pose and angle heuristics reliable enough for a small set of common exercises.

### Idea 2: ReturnClock

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Rocket Money — proved people will pay to stop silent money leaks when the payoff is an instant dollars-at-risk reveal

**Blocks used:** Camera - Calendar - Consequences

**Concept:** Screenshot order confirmations, return-policy pages, or shipping emails, and the app extracts the exact return deadline, refund amount at risk, and any restocking fee before the window closes. The loop is save the proof once, watch the countdown, then get escalating reminders when you are near the last day or near a likely drop-off point. It copies the Rocket Money anxiety model but applies it to shopping regret, which is more frequent and more visual than subscription cleanup.

**Reveal moment:** A blunt screen says "Return these shoes by Sunday 5:00 PM or lose $86" with a live countdown and the exact refund you are about to forfeit.

**The hook:** It turns "I should probably return that later" into a visible money-loss clock you cannot ignore.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is robustly parsing messy merchant screenshots, so v1 should focus on the biggest email and checkout formats first.

### Idea 3: WalkIn Score

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved people will pay for a blunt appearance verdict and a clear "fix this first" improvement path

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - Share

**Concept:** Prop your phone up, record a 5-second walk-in video before a date, interview, or party, and the app scores posture, smile timing, chin angle, shoulder openness, and overall first-impression presence. The loop is record, get a First Impression Score, retake with one correction, and compare the before-and-after clips. It is a better version of selfie-rating apps because it judges the three seconds people actually experience, not a frozen face.

**Reveal moment:** The app freezes the exact second your first-impression score peaks, stamps it "This is when you looked most attractive," and shows the slouch or awkward frame that killed the rest of the clip.

**The hook:** It answers the question mirrors cannot: what do I look like in the first three seconds other people see me?

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is guided framing and motion scoring that feels believable without turning v1 into a full computer-vision research project.
