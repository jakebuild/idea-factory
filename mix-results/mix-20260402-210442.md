### Idea 1: Glow Clash

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** Cal AI — reportedly does $3M+/mo, proved that "scan what you're about to consume, get one scary verdict instantly" is a brutally effective conversion model

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - Consequences

**Concept:** You scan the skincare products you plan to use tonight, and the app flags ingredient collisions that commonly lead to redness, peeling, breakouts, or next-morning irritation before a date, event, or photo day. The core loop is simple: scan your stack, get a blunt irritation verdict, swap one product, and come back because most people rotate the same acids, retinoids, and spot treatments in slightly risky combinations.

**Reveal moment:** A red card flashes, "High irritation risk: retinol + AHA toner + benzoyl peroxide means your face is likely to look worse by morning, not better."

**The hook:** It catches the expensive skincare routine that is quietly sabotaging your face the night before you need to look good.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is normalizing messy ingredient labels into a reliable conflict database without making manual correction annoying.

### Idea 2: Grind Receipt

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** SnoreLab — proved people will pay for overnight audio analysis when the morning payoff is hard evidence they can hear and compare

**Blocks used:** Microphone - Pattern learning - Streak

**Concept:** You leave your phone by the bed, and the app detects likely teeth-grinding bursts and jaw-clenching episodes overnight, then shows your total grind minutes, the worst audio clip, and the nights your pattern is getting worse. The loop is sleep, get the receipt, try one intervention like stress reduction, magnesium, or a night guard, and compare because jaw pain and dental damage are recurring anxieties, not one-off curiosities.

**Reveal moment:** "You had 31 grinding bursts last night, and your worst 12-minute window was 4:08 AM to 4:20 AM."

**The hook:** It gives people proof for the problem their partner or dentist keeps warning them about but they have never actually seen.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is making overnight audio detection conservative enough to feel useful without pretending to diagnose anything medical.

### Idea 3: Cancel Window

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Rocket Money — proved deadline-plus-dollars-at-risk screens convert because invisible losses feel urgent once the app makes them explicit

**Blocks used:** Photos - Object recognition - Consequences

**Concept:** Screenshot hotel bookings, Airbnb reservations, event tickets, or car rentals, and the app extracts the exact free-cancel deadline, penalty amount, and the last moment you can change your mind without getting burned. The core loop is save the booking proof once, watch the countdown, and get escalating reminders before the cancellation window slams shut, which makes it Rocket Money for travel and plans instead of subscriptions.

**Reveal moment:** A blunt screen says, "Cancel this hotel by Thursday 6:00 PM or lose $214."

**The hook:** It turns vague trip admin into one visible money-loss clock before a forgotten booking quietly becomes a charge.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is extracting cancellation rules from messy booking confirmations without overpromising accuracy on edge-case policies.
