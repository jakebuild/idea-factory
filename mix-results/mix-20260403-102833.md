### Idea 1: Authority Receipt

**Targets:** Status

**Copies:** Crystal Knows — proved people pay for AI communication analysis when the payoff is a blunt verdict on how they come across before a high-stakes message goes out

**Blocks used:** Text input - Score reveal - AI rewrite

**Concept:** Paste a Slack message, negotiation email, performance self-review, or follow-up note, and the app scores how deferential, scattered, or authority-draining it sounds. Then it rewrites the message in a sharper version and highlights the exact phrases making you sound junior, so the loop becomes paste, get humbled, send the stronger draft, repeat before every important work message.

**Reveal moment:** A red card says, "This message scores 3/10 on authority because you apologized twice, buried the ask, and gave away your leverage," then shows the upgraded version beside it.

**The hook:** It tells ambitious people whether their own writing is quietly making them look lower-status at work.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is constraining the scoring rubric so the feedback feels sharp and repeatable instead of like generic rewrite AI.

### Idea 2: ClaimKit

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** NEXT Insurance — proved people will pay for instant claim-ready document generation when the real relief is turning messy evidence into a usable packet in seconds

**Blocks used:** OCR - PDF export - Templates

**Concept:** Upload a purchase receipt, damage photo, shipping email, and merchant thread, and the app assembles the exact refund, warranty, or chargeback packet you need instead of making you hunt through inboxes and forms. It extracts dates, prices, merchant terms, and missing evidence, then gives you a ready-to-send email and attachment bundle so the loop becomes upload, recover money, save the next failed purchase faster.

**Reveal moment:** A blunt screen says, "You still have 11 days of warranty coverage left and your claim packet is ready to send."

**The hook:** It turns the refund process people procrastinate until they lose the money into a one-tap recovery kit.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is keeping the claim templates narrow enough in v1 that they feel reliable without trying to cover every merchant edge case.

### Idea 3: First-Date Voice

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** Poised — proved people will pay for rapid speaking feedback when the outcome is sounding more confident and appealing in high-stakes conversations

**Blocks used:** Microphone - Score reveal - Retake

**Concept:** You record your first 15-20 seconds for a date opener, voice note, or "tell me about yourself" answer, and the app scores warmth, pace, monotony, dead-air risk, and flirt energy. Then it gives one retake instruction like "slow down 12% and smile through the first sentence," so the loop becomes record, improve one thing, rerun, and walk into the interaction sounding more magnetic.

**Reveal moment:** Your first take lands at 4.4/10 chemistry, the retake jumps to 7.2/10, and the app highlights the exact second your pace stopped killing the vibe.

**The hook:** It gives people a before-and-after attraction receipt before they send the voice note or show up to the date.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is keeping the scoring focused on a few defensible audio signals instead of pretending to measure full romantic chemistry.
