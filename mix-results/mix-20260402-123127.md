### Idea 1: VenueProof

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved people will pay for a blunt attractiveness verdict plus a clear "fix this first" path

**Blocks used:** Camera - Light sensor - Object recognition

**Concept:** Before a date, party, or selfie, the user pans the room with their phone and the app finds the exact spot, angle, and camera height where they look best under the actual lighting. It compares the user's current view to the optimized one, then gives one blunt instruction like "half-step left" or "raise camera to eye level." It builds a better version of the UMax model by moving from passive scoring to in-the-moment positioning.

**Reveal moment:** One tap shows "Best face spot found" and overlays the exact place in the room where you look better than where you are standing now.

**The hook:** It literally tells you where to stand in the room to look hotter in photos.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is making face and lighting scoring feel stable enough in real rooms without building full AR complexity.

### Idea 2: Renewal Radar

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Rocket Money — proved people will pay to stop silent recurring charges when the payoff is an instant money-leak reveal

**Blocks used:** Photos - LLM - Consequences

**Concept:** The user uploads screenshots of trial signups, App Store subscription pages, or renewal emails, and the app extracts the charge amount, renewal date, cancellation deadline, and any "switches to annual after X days" trap. It turns those into countdown cards with escalating alerts and a simple cancel-prep checklist, so the whole product is about preventing specific future losses rather than doing full budgeting. It builds a tighter, scarier version of the Rocket Money model by focusing only on the moment before money leaves your account.

**Reveal moment:** The first scan says, "You are about to lose $118 in the next 9 days unless you cancel these 3 trials now."

**The hook:** It catches the charge before your bank statement does.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is handling messy screenshot and email OCR reliably enough that users trust the extracted renewal details.

### Idea 3: Burn Window

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Cal AI — proved people will repeatedly scan real-world inputs if the payoff is an instant, scary number tied to today's body

**Blocks used:** GPS - Weather - Consequences

**Concept:** The user picks or scans their SPF once, shares location, and logs how long they plan to be outside, and the app calculates their Burn Window, reapply deadline, and "face-safe until" time based on UV, cloud cover, and skin sensitivity. The loop is simple: check before going out, see the countdown, then either protect yourself or accept the cost. It builds a better version of the Cal AI model by replacing calorie anxiety with sun-damage anxiety and a much clearer deadline.

**Reveal moment:** The app tells you, "At this UV, your face starts burning at 1:42 PM and your SPF stops protecting you at 12:55 PM."

**The hook:** It turns vague sunscreen guilt into an exact countdown to when your skin starts losing.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is keeping the model simple and believable enough that users act on it without expecting medical-grade precision.
