### Idea 1: SmileReceipt

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved blunt appearance scoring plus one clear fix is strong enough to drive paid subscriptions and shareable verdict screens

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - AI-generated images

**Concept:** You take three guided smile selfies: neutral, teeth showing, and side angle. The app scores teeth brightness, gum show, symmetry, and lip framing, then generates light-touch smile variants so it can tell you whether whitening, a closed-mouth smile, or a different angle would improve your first impression most. The core loop is simple: test before a date, profile update, or photo dump, save the winning smile style, then rerun after dental work or whitening.

**Reveal moment:** Three versions of your smile appear side by side and one is stamped, "Best Smile: +9 first-impression points over your usual grin."

**The hook:** It answers the insecurity most people have but never get objective feedback on: whether their smile is helping or hurting their attractiveness.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is keeping smile analysis and light-touch image edits consistent enough to feel trustworthy.

### Idea 2: PillClash

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Cal AI — reportedly doing $3M+/mo, proved that "scan it, get one scary health verdict instantly" is a powerful mobile conversion model

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - Consequences

**Concept:** You scan the supplements, cold medicine, sleep gummies, and pre-workout you are about to take, and the app totals overlaps like caffeine, melatonin, magnesium, acetaminophen, and antihistamines across your whole day. The onboarding sells one fear: stop accidentally stacking products that make you groggy, wired, or sick. The core loop is scan before you swallow, get a blunt safe-or-not verdict, then come back because most people reuse the same few products in slightly risky combinations.

**Reveal moment:** A red card flashes, "This stack puts you at 480mg caffeine, double melatonin, and a high drowsiness risk before your drive home."

**The hook:** It catches the accidental self-sabotage people do with totally normal products sitting in their bathroom and kitchen.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is normalizing messy supplement and medicine labels into a clean ingredient model without making manual correction painful.

### Idea 3: FreshClock

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Rocket Money — proved deadline-plus-dollars-at-risk reveals make invisible losses feel urgent enough to pay to avoid

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - Consequences

**Concept:** You snap a leftover container, grocery pack, or takeout receipt, and the app turns it into one exact eat-by deadline based on food type, storage method, and when it entered the fridge. The core loop is save food in under five seconds, watch the live spoil clock, and get escalating alerts before it becomes trash or a bad gamble. It is Rocket Money for fridge waste: not "track meals," but "here is the money and stomach pain you are about to lose if you forget this."

**Reveal moment:** A blunt screen says, "Eat this chicken by tomorrow 1:10 PM or toss $18 and risk a bad night."

**The hook:** It turns fridge guilt into a visible countdown before your leftovers quietly become both waste and a health risk.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is mapping scanned foods into conservative, trustworthy food-safety windows without overcomplicating the flow.
