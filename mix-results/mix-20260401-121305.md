### Idea 1: Ghost Signal

**Targets:** Sex / Dating

**Copies:** Rizz — proved people will pay for AI dating reply help because the real pain is not "getting matches," it's not knowing what to text next

**Blocks used:** Photos - LLM - Share

**Concept:** You upload a screenshot of a dating chat or iMessage thread. The app scores the conversation's momentum, tells you whether they're genuinely interested or politely fading, highlights the exact line that hurt the vibe, and gives you 3 reply options from safe to bold. The core loop is simple: screenshot, get the verdict, send the fix, come back after the next reply.

**Reveal moment:** "Reply odds: 31% - you turned a flirt into an interview by asking three questions in a row."

**The hook:** It tells you in five seconds whether you're being ghosted and what to send next.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — hardest part is reliable OCR and chat parsing across messy screenshot formats.

### Idea 2: Crash Score

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Cal AI — proved people will repeatedly scan meals if the payoff is an instant number reveal; Cal AI reportedly does $3M+/mo with photo-based food analysis

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - Steps

**Concept:** You photograph your meal and the app predicts not just calories, but how likely it is to cause an afternoon energy crash. It gives every meal a "Crash Score," then suggests the smallest post-meal walk that offsets it, turning each scan into an immediate behavior change instead of passive tracking. Over time it learns your patterns and says things like "rice bowls usually hit you harder than wraps."

**Reveal moment:** "Crash Score 8/10: this lunch is likely to wreck your energy by 2:37 PM unless you walk 1,200 steps after eating."

**The hook:** It doesn't count calories, it tells you whether your lunch is about to ruin your afternoon.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — hardest part is making the first version feel credible without overclaiming medical precision, so v1 should use clear heuristic language.

### Idea 3: Fridge Rescue

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Flashfood — proved consumers respond hard to visible food savings; the winning model is turning waste avoidance into a concrete dollar number

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - Challenge

**Concept:** You scan your fridge and pantry, and the app estimates how much money is at risk of being thrown away this week. It spots the items most likely to expire first, generates 3 rescue meals from what you already have, and keeps a no-waste streak so there is a reason to come back after the novelty wears off. The loop is nightly scan, pick a rescue, save the item, protect the streak.

**Reveal moment:** "$26.40 at risk by Friday - spinach, yogurt, and berries; tap to cook the fastest rescue meal."

**The hook:** It turns vague guilt about food waste into a brutally specific dollar-loss countdown.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — hardest part is loose expiry estimation for unlabeled produce, but v1 can stay useful with conservative defaults and user confirmation.
