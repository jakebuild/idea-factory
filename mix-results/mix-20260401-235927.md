### Idea 1: Caffeine Curfew

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Cal AI — proved people will repeatedly scan what they consume if the payoff is an instant, scary number reveal; the prompt notes Cal AI does $3M+/mo with photo-based food analysis

**Blocks used:** Camera - Sleep - Heart rate

**Concept:** You scan a coffee, energy drink, pre-workout tub, or cafe menu item and the app predicts tonight's sleep damage before you buy it. The loop is brutally simple: scan caffeine, see your likely sleep-onset time and tomorrow's energy hit, then swap to a safer option or accept the damage and track whether the app was right. It is Cal AI for the fear that one bad drink will wreck your night and tomorrow's face, focus, and mood.

**Reveal moment:** "This 3:40 PM cold brew likely pushes sleep onset to 1:08 AM and drops tomorrow's energy to 4/10."

**The hook:** It tells you if this drink is stealing tomorrow before you even order it.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is making the prediction feel credible with a narrow caffeine-plus-bedtime rules engine instead of fake medical precision.

### Idea 2: Return Clock

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Rocket Money — proved people pay to stop silent money leaks when the app turns vague loss into one blunt number and a deadline

**Blocks used:** Camera - Calendar - Consequences

**Concept:** You scan a receipt or order email, the app extracts the store, item price, and return deadline, then starts a live countdown showing exactly how much money is about to become unrecoverable. The loop is scan purchase, watch the "money at risk" total, get escalating alerts in the final 72 hours, and protect your streak of saved dollars by returning or keeping items deliberately. It sells loss prevention, not budgeting virtue.

**Reveal moment:** "$148 becomes non-refundable in 2 days 11 hours - start your Target return now or lose it."

**The hook:** It makes forgotten returns feel like cash evaporating in real time.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is parsing messy receipts and retailer return windows well enough that the deadline feels trustworthy.

### Idea 3: Smile Receipt

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved people will pay for a blunt appearance verdict and a clear "fix this first" path

**Blocks used:** Camera - Light sensor - Streak

**Concept:** You do a guided 5-second smile scan in consistent lighting and the app scores tooth brightness, gum redness, and "clean smile" quality against your own best baseline. The loop is morning scan, blunt score reveal, one action like skip coffee rinse, whitening strip, or brush timing, then come back because smile anxiety is repetitive and photo-driven. It is a narrower, more believable vanity product than generic face rating because the user can see exactly what changed.

**Reveal moment:** "Smile Score 62 - your front teeth look 1.7 shades duller than your best week, mostly from surface staining."

**The hook:** It tells you whether your smile actually looks worse today or you are just overthinking selfies.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is enforcing consistent framing and lighting so week-to-week comparisons do not feel random.
