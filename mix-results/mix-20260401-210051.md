### Idea 1: Hairline Receipt

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved people will pay for a blunt appearance verdict and a clear "fix this first" upsell

**Blocks used:** Camera - Face aging - Streak

**Concept:** A weekly guided selfie scan measures temple depth, crown visibility, and overall hairline change against your own baseline instead of a generic beauty score. The loop is obsessive in the right way: scan from the same angles, get proof of change or no change, see a 6-month projection, then follow one simple action for the next week. It is UMax for the specific insecurity that keeps people checking bathroom mirrors and old photos.

**Reveal moment:** "Temple recession +1.8 mm in 5 weeks - here is your August projection if nothing changes."

**The hook:** It tells you whether your hairline is actually moving or whether you are just spiraling.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is enforcing consistent selfie framing so the comparison feels trustworthy instead of random.

### Idea 2: Hydration Deadline

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Waterllama — proved people will open a hydration app every day when the loop is simple, streaky, and emotionally sticky; the prompt notes 6 million users and 149k reviews

**Blocks used:** Weather - Steps - Consequences

**Concept:** Instead of counting cups, the app predicts the exact time today you are likely to hit dehydration symptoms based on local heat, your step count, and what you have already logged drinking. Each drink pushes the deadline later, while missing your target cracks a visible "safe day" streak, so the loop becomes check the deadline, drink to move it, keep the streak alive. The differentiation is that it sells prevention of a bad afternoon, not generic wellness virtue.

**Reveal moment:** "At your current pace, headache risk hits at 2:50 PM - drink 22 oz now to push it past 6:10 PM."

**The hook:** It turns "drink more water" into a live countdown to feeling bad.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is making the risk model feel believable with a narrow rules-based v1 instead of fake precision.

### Idea 3: Spiral Forecast

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Remy AI — proved people want personalized health coaching that explains the why, not just raw logs

**Blocks used:** Sleep - Calendar - LLM

**Concept:** You do a 10-second morning check-in, connect sleep data, and let the app read today's calendar to predict when your stress or anxiety is most likely to spike. The core loop is morning forecast, one pre-emptive action, then an evening "was that accurate?" check so the model gets more personal over time. It is a tighter, more actionable version of mood tracking because it sells prevention instead of reflection.

**Reveal moment:** "Highest spiral risk today: 4:30-5:15 PM, driven by poor sleep plus back-to-back meetings - start a 2-minute reset now."

**The hook:** It tells you when today is most likely to emotionally go sideways before it happens.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is keeping the insights concrete and useful without implying clinical accuracy the app cannot support in v1.
