### Idea 2: CredentialCliff

**Targets:** Fear of Loss / Fear of Losing Income

**Copies:** NEXT Insurance — $548M in 2024 revenue, proved workers and small business owners pay quickly when paperwork gaps threaten their livelihood

**Blocks used:** OCR document scan - Expiration countdown - Home screen widget

**Concept:** CredentialCliff is for nurses, bartenders, cosmetologists, contractors, real-estate agents, and other licensed workers who lose shifts when a permit quietly expires. You scan each license, certification, or permit once, and the app extracts the expiration date, renewal steps, and supporting docs, then turns it into a blunt income-risk dashboard with countdowns and escalating reminders. What makes it unique is the framing: not document storage, but how many workdays are exposed if you ignore this.

**Reveal moment:** You open the app and see a red card that says, "Food handler permit expires in 11 days. Two scheduled shifts next month depend on it."

**The hook:** It turns boring renewal paperwork into an explicit countdown to lost money.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + OCR, reminders, and a rules-based dashboard are solo-dev realistic; the hardest part is handling messy state-by-state document formats without brittle parsing.

### Idea 3: DoseGarden

**Targets:** Health / Fear of Backsliding

**Copies:** Water tracker Waterllama — 149,549 reviews and 6M users, proved collectible characters plus streak mechanics can make a boring health habit sticky

**Blocks used:** Recovery pet - Medication reminders - Weekly progress photos

**Concept:** DoseGarden is for people who keep quitting meds or treatments because the payoff is delayed and invisible in week one. You pick a track like acne, hair loss, blood pressure, supplements, or ADHD meds, then log each dose and optional weekly photo or symptom check-in; the app grows a recovery creature and shows a simple "likely payoff window" based on your streak. The core loop is take the dose, protect the creature, hit the first visible-result milestone, and come back because missing days now feels like undoing progress you can actually see.

**Reveal moment:** On day 17, the app flips from "too early to judge" to "this is the first week your treatment should start showing up" and unlocks your first side-by-side progress card.

**The hook:** It makes boring adherence feel like a game until you realize the game is your actual skin, focus, or blood pressure.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + reminders, streaks, a small protocol library, and simple photo comparisons are all feasible; the hardest part is choosing a narrow enough set of treatment timelines to avoid fake precision.
