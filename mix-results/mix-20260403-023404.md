### Idea 1: Cough Receipt

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** SnoreLab — proved people will pay for overnight audio analysis when the payoff is a blunt morning receipt with proof they can hear

**Blocks used:** Microphone - Pattern learning - Challenge

**Concept:** You leave your phone on the nightstand, and by morning the app shows total cough bursts, the rough time clusters, and the worst 10-second clip from the night. Then it gives one simple 2-3 night experiment like humidifier on, window closed, or earlier allergy meds so the loop becomes sleep, get the receipt, test one change, compare tomorrow. It is a better version of generic symptom logs because it gives proof first and advice second.

**Reveal moment:** A blunt morning card says, "You coughed 47 times last night, and 61% of it happened between 3:10 AM and 4:00 AM."

**The hook:** It gives you proof for the night problem you keep feeling but can never measure clearly enough to fix or show a doctor.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is making overnight cough detection conservative enough to feel trustworthy without drifting into fake medical diagnosis.

### Idea 2: Laundry Rescue

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Alarmy — proved forced-action reminders with real consequences convert better than soft nudges because people pay to avoid repeat pain

**Blocks used:** Microphone - Consequences - Streak

**Concept:** Start a washer or dryer timer in one tap, or let the app listen for the end beep pattern once you train it. When the load finishes, the app starts a visible mildew-or-rewrash countdown and escalates alerts until you confirm you moved the clothes, turning "I'll get it in a minute" into a real penalty clock. It is Alarmy for laundry procrastination instead of waking up.

**Reveal moment:** The lock-screen countdown flips red and says, "This wet load has sat for 82 minutes and is now in rewash-risk territory."

**The hook:** It turns one of the dumbest recurring household losses into a clock you cannot quietly ignore.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is making beep training and timer fallback simple enough that setup does not kill the habit.

### Idea 3: BiteBack

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Cal AI — proved people will repeatedly scan what they are about to consume if the payoff is an instant, scary health verdict; Cal AI reportedly does $3M+/mo with photo-based food analysis

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - Consequences

**Concept:** You scan the dessert, soda, sports drink, or late-night snack you are about to have, tell the app roughly when you will brush, and it calculates a Tooth Damage Window based on sugar, acidity, stickiness, and timing. Instead of generic nutrition advice, onboarding sells one fear: stop casually buying cavities and enamel damage at night when your mouth is most vulnerable. The core loop is scan, see the damage window, swap the snack or change brushing timing, then come back because the same nightly choices repeat.

**Reveal moment:** A red screen says, "This bedtime snack keeps your teeth in cavity-risk mode until 11:42 PM and your safe-brush time starts in 34 minutes."

**The hook:** It tells you whether your "small treat" tonight is quietly turning into a dentist bill later.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is mapping scanned foods into simple, credible dental-risk heuristics without pretending to deliver clinical precision.
