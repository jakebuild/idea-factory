### Idea 1: Refill Clock

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Rocket Money — proved people pay for a blunt deadline-and-dollars-at-risk reveal when the pain is silent until it is too late

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - Consequences

**Concept:** You photograph a prescription label, enter roughly how many pills are left, and the app calculates your exact run-out date, refill lead time, and the risk window if you wait. The core loop is simple: scan once, adjust pill count in one tap, then get escalating alerts before you hit the "Sunday night and no meds left" panic point. It is different from generic med reminders because it sells one thing in onboarding: never get surprised by a refill cliff again.

**Reveal moment:** A blunt screen says, "You will run out of Lexapro in 6 days and your refill usually needs 3 business days."

**The hook:** It kills one of the most common adult panic moments by turning a vague refill worry into one exact date you cannot ignore.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is making OCR reliable across messy prescription labels and keeping the manual correction flow fast.

### Idea 2: CutScore

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved blunt attractiveness scoring plus a clear "improve this one thing" loop is strong enough to drive paid viral sharing

**Blocks used:** Camera - AI-generated images - Object recognition

**Concept:** You upload one clean selfie and the app generates a small set of realistic haircut and beard variants, then scores each one for jaw definition, face balance, and age impression. The core loop is test styles before a barber visit, save the winning reference, then come back every time your hair grows out. What makes it stronger than generic hairstyle try-on apps is that it does not just show options; it tells you which one actually improves your face most.

**Reveal moment:** Four versions of your face appear side by side and one is stamped, "Best Cut: +11 attractiveness points."

**The hook:** It answers the question most men never get a real answer to before a haircut: which style makes me look better, not just different.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is keeping generated hairstyle previews consistent enough with the user's real face to feel trustworthy.

### Idea 3: StackCheck

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Cal AI — reportedly doing $3M+/mo, proved that "scan object, get one health verdict instantly" is a high-converting mobile model

**Blocks used:** Camera - Object recognition - Habits

**Concept:** You scan your supplement bottles, pre-workout tubs, and energy drinks, and the app totals key ingredients across your whole day: caffeine, magnesium, vitamin D, zinc, melatonin, and more. The core loop is scan what you are about to take, see if today's stack is safe or wasteful, then come back because most people have no idea when they are accidentally double-dosing. It wins by giving a sharper answer than generic supplement trackers: not "log your vitamins," but "this combo is pushing you over the line."

**Reveal moment:** The app flashes, "Today's stack puts you at 540mg caffeine and 3x your zinc target before 2 PM."

**The hook:** It catches the accidental overdoses and useless supplement overlap people do not realize they are paying for.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is normalizing supplement-label OCR into a clean ingredient model without forcing too much manual cleanup.
