### Idea 1: VenueProof

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved people will pay for a blunt attractiveness verdict and a clear "fix this first" path

**Blocks used:** Camera - Light sensor - Object recognition

**Concept:** Before a date, event, or selfie, the user pans the room and the app finds the exact standing spot, camera height, and angle where they will look best under the actual lighting. It compares the current frame to the optimized frame, gives one blunt instruction like "half-step left" or "raise camera to eye level," and turns appearance anxiety into an immediate before-vs-after win. This builds a better version of the UMax model by moving from passive scoring to live correction in the real world.

**Reveal moment:** One tap shows "Best face spot found" and overlays the exact place in the room where you look better than where you are standing now.

**The hook:** It literally tells you where to stand in the room to look hotter in photos.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is making the lighting and framing score feel stable enough in messy real rooms without building full AR complexity.

### Idea 2: Burn Window

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Cal AI — proved people will repeatedly scan real-world inputs when the payoff is an instant, scary number reveal tied to their body

**Blocks used:** GPS - Weather - Consequences

**Concept:** The user picks their SPF, skin sensitivity, and how long they plan to be outside, and the app calculates their Burn Window, reapply deadline, and "face-safe until" time using live UV and weather data. The loop is check before going out, see the countdown, then either protect yourself or accept the damage, which makes it feel like Cal AI for sun anxiety instead of calories. It is differentiated from generic UV apps because the product is not educational tracking, it is a blunt personal deadline.

**Reveal moment:** The app says, "At this UV, your face starts burning at 1:42 PM and your sunscreen stops protecting you at 12:55 PM."

**The hook:** It turns vague sunscreen guilt into an exact countdown to when your skin starts losing.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is keeping the model simple and believable enough that users act on it without expecting medical-grade precision.

### Idea 3: TowProof

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Rocket Money — proved people will pay to avoid silent money leaks when the payoff is an instant "you are about to lose money" reveal

**Blocks used:** Camera - GPS - LLM

**Concept:** When the user parks, they scan the parking sign stack and the app translates it into one exact move-by deadline, the likely ticket risk, and a countdown alarm. Instead of making people decode impossible street-sign logic, it gives a blunt answer like "safe until 7:58 AM tomorrow" and keeps nagging before the penalty window starts. This copies the Rocket Money model of exposing a specific future loss, but applies it to parking anxiety where the pain is immediate and easy to screen-record.

**Reveal moment:** After one scan, the app says, "Move your car by 7:58 AM tomorrow or risk a $95 ticket."

**The hook:** It turns unreadable parking signs into one exact deadline before the city takes your money.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is parsing messy multi-sign rules reliably enough that users trust the first answer.
