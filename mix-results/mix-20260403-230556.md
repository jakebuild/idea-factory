### Idea 1: AgeScore

**Targets:** Financial Anxiety / Fear of Inadequacy

**Copies:** Credit Karma — 130M users, $1.8B acquisition, proved that people will obsessively check a free score that makes them feel financially behind

**Blocks used:** Simple data input - peer percentile score reveal - weekly re-engagement loop

**Concept:** You enter your age, current savings, and monthly income. AgeScore instantly shows your Retirement Readiness Score (0–100) and where you rank vs your real age cohort using Federal Reserve benchmark data. The reveal is always slightly scary — most people are behind. Weekly score-check reminders pull you back and show whether the gap is closing or widening.

**Reveal moment:** "At 31, your retirement readiness score is 38/100 — you're in the 29th percentile for your age group."

**The hook:** Most people have a vague dread about retirement savings. This turns the dread into a number — and a number you can improve.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is sourcing reliable age-cohort savings benchmarks, but Federal Reserve Survey of Consumer Finances data is public and directly usable.

### Idea 2: ResumeRank

**Targets:** Career Status Anxiety / Professional Insecurity

**Copies:** Photofeeler — ~$2–3M ARR, proved professionals will repeatedly pay for a numerical score on their professional presentation

**Blocks used:** Text/file input - AI percentile analysis - targeted improvement suggestions

**Concept:** Paste your resume and select a target job title. ResumeRank scores your resume against the language patterns of successful job postings and career resources for that role. You get a percentile rank (e.g., 44th percentile for Senior PM roles) plus 3 concrete changes to move up. Weekly re-score lets you track progress as you iterate.

**Reveal moment:** "Your resume scores 44th percentile for Senior Product Manager roles — your biggest gap is missing quantified impact metrics."

**The hook:** Most resume feedback is vague ("make it stronger!"). This gives you a number and tells you exactly what's keeping you out of the top half.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is building a scoring rubric that feels credible; using GPT-4o against a curated set of job description patterns is achievable in 2 weeks.

### Idea 3: DayReady

**Targets:** Athletic Identity / Fear of Wasted Training

**Copies:** Whoop — $3.6B valuation, proved athletes pay for a daily readiness number that tells them how hard to push

**Blocks used:** Morning questionnaire - AI readiness score - workout type recommendation

**Concept:** No wearable required. Each morning: 5 quick inputs (sleep hours, sleep quality, mood, soreness, stress level). DayReady generates a 0–100 Readiness Score and tells you today's training zone — push hard, moderate effort, active recovery, or full rest. Over time it learns your patterns and shows your 30-day readiness trend.

**Reveal moment:** "Today's readiness: 58/100 — your nervous system is taxed. Do 30 minutes of zone 2 cardio, not your planned leg day."

**The hook:** Whoop costs $30/month and needs a wearable. DayReady gives you the same daily training decision in 30 seconds, free.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is making the score feel accurate enough to trust; calibrate against user-reported workout outcomes in early versions to improve confidence.
