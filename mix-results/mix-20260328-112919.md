### Idea 1: WeekPrint

**Blocks used:** Calendar data - Procedural generation - Share

**Concept:** WeekPrint pulls your Apple/Google Calendar and turns your week into a unique generative art poster. Each event category maps to a color, weight, and texture — a week full of back-to-back meetings becomes dense geometric grids, a weekend of free time becomes open watercolor washes. Every Sunday you get a new piece of art that is literally your week. One tap to save or share.

**Why it works:** Your data IS the content. No one has to create anything — your schedule already exists, it just needs a beautiful frame. The art is unique to each person's week, making sharing feel personal rather than performative. Works the same formula as Dream Landscape (scored 8/10) but for the calendar, which is the one dataset almost everyone maintains.

**The hook:** "My coworker showed me what her nightmare sprint week looked like as art — it was stunning and horrifying at the same time."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — hardest part is the EventKit/Google Calendar OAuth scope approval, which can take a few days; the generative art logic itself is straightforward with Canvas/SVG rules.

### Idea 2: SoundLayer

**Blocks used:** Microphone - Streak - Abstract/brush strokes art - Collect

**Concept:** Each day you tap once and SoundLayer records 10 seconds of your environment — morning coffee shop, evening walk, quiet desk. The app analyzes frequency, volume, and rhythm and paints a new abstract brushstroke layer onto your weekly canvas. By day 7, you have a one-of-a-kind painting made entirely from the sounds of your life. Tap any layer to replay the original recording underneath it.

**Why it works:** Collection + streak + "your data IS content." The daily action is trivial (one tap, 10 seconds) but the output accumulates into something genuinely beautiful. The playback feature — tap a layer and hear the original sound — is the surprise moment that makes people demo it immediately. The painting is also a private sonic diary, which adds emotional weight beyond typical shareable content.

**The hook:** "You can tap any color in the painting and hear the exact sound that created it — I tapped the quiet grey layer and heard my kid sleeping."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — microphone recording + basic FFT analysis for frequency mapping + Canvas layer compositing. The hard part is making the visual output look beautiful across wildly different sound inputs; needs careful tuning of the mapping rules.

### Idea 3: Life Constellation

**Blocks used:** GPS - Constellations art - Collect - Share

**Concept:** Life Constellation turns your movement patterns into a personal star map. Every place you visit logs as a star — the more you go there, the brighter and larger it glows. Paths between locations become constellation lines. Over a week your life draws a unique constellation: the gym and coffee shop close together form one cluster, your commute traces a straight line of dim stars, home is the brightest point. Each week's constellation is saved to your collection, and you can name the shapes you see in them.

**Why it works:** Pure "your data IS the content" — no input required, it's already happening. The constellation metaphor transforms mundane location data into something poetic and personal. Every person's map looks completely different, which drives sharing. Weekly collection gives a reason to return. Close in formula to Dream Landscape (8/10) but taps movement instead of sleep — and movement data is something everyone already has without needing to log anything.

**The hook:** "I looked at my constellation for last week and realized I only had three stars — I hadn't gone anywhere except home, work, and the grocery store. It was a wake-up call."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — Core Location background updates + a simple distance-clustering algorithm + Canvas drawing for the star/line rendering. The hardest part is battery-efficient background location without burning through GPS; use significant-location-change mode rather than continuous GPS.
