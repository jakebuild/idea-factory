### Idea 1: Aesthetic Type

**Targets:** Identity

**Copies:** 16Personalities — proven that tens of millions of people crave a named personality type they can share; free + premium model generates estimated $20M+ ARR and massive organic social sharing

**Blocks used:** Camera (camera roll access) - AI image analysis (vision API) - AI art generation (DALL-E / Flux)

**Concept:** Tap to analyze 12 recent photos from your camera roll. The AI scans your recurring visual patterns — dominant colors, recurring subjects, mood and lighting — and assigns you a named Aesthetic Type like "Golden Hour Romantic" or "Urban Minimalist." The output is a single shareable card: your type name, a one-line description, and an AI-generated mood board built from your visual identity. No quiz, no self-reporting — your photos already know who you are.

**Reveal moment:** Your custom Aesthetic Type name and a generated mood board that perfectly captures your visual personality appear in one shareable card after a 3-second scan.

**The hook:** "This app read my entire personality from 12 iPhone photos and it was uncomfortably accurate."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — camera roll access + vision API for theme extraction + image generation for mood board. Hardest part: making type names feel resonant and non-generic across a diverse set of users.

### Idea 2: Rizz Score

**Targets:** Sex / Dating

**Copies:** Crystal Knows — proved $10M+ ARR that people will pay for AI-powered communication analysis; combined with Photofeeler's model of external rating validation before a date

**Blocks used:** LLM (conversation analysis) - Score reveal - Single-action fix mechanic

**Concept:** Paste your last 10–15 messages with a match into the app. The AI scores your "Rizz" across four dimensions — confidence, intrigue, availability, and wit — then gives you one specific, blunt thing to change to increase your reply rate. Works for Hinge, Bumble, Tinder, and Instagram DMs. No account needed, no data stored, results in under 5 seconds.

**Reveal moment:** "Your Rizz Score is 4/10 — you're double-texting and answering in under 2 minutes. One fix: next message, wait 4 hours."

**The hook:** "My Rizz Score came back a 3/10 and it fixed my Hinge reply rate overnight."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — LLM prompt engineering + minimal UI. Zero external API dependencies beyond a language model. Hardest part: calibrating the scoring so it feels honest and not easily gamed by pasting perfect messages.

### Idea 3: Receipt Regret

**Targets:** Wealth

**Copies:** Rocket Money ($80M+ ARR — proved people pay to confront where money leaks) + Cal AI's scan-for-instant-reveal model ($3M+/mo — proved camera-based reveals drive daily habit)

**Blocks used:** Camera (receipt scan + OCR) - Compound interest math engine - Score reveal (monthly Opportunity Cost)

**Concept:** Scan any receipt or type any purchase amount. The app shows you the "Real Cost" — what that exact money compounds to in 10, 20, and 30 years at 7% average market returns. It doesn't lecture you; it just shows you the number. Accumulates into a monthly Opportunity Cost Score so you can watch what your spending month is actually worth in retirement terms.

**Reveal moment:** "That $340 coffee month = $2,550 of retirement money. Your November Opportunity Cost: $1,240."

**The hook:** "I scanned one oat milk latte and saw it was costing me $90 in retirement money — I switched to drip the next day."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — OCR API for receipt parsing (or manual amount entry as fallback) + compound interest formula + simple score accumulation. Hardest part: receipt OCR accuracy across varied formats; manual entry fallback eliminates this as a blocker.
