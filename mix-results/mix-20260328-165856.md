### Idea 1: ShakeShot

**Blocks used:** Calendar (Data) - Shake (Physical Interaction) - AI Image Generation - Consequences

**Concept:** Every morning you get exactly ONE shake to generate your AI "day portrait" — an image synthesized from your calendar events for that day (meeting titles, number of events, busy vs. open). Shake hard = bold, chaotic art style. Shake gently = calm, minimal. No reshakes. You're locked in. At day's end, you caption it with one word for what the day actually felt like and share the prediction-vs-reality pair.

**Why it works:** Consequences + your data IS the content + shareable moments. The no-redo rule creates genuine stakes (Alarmy's core insight: consequences beat motivation). The prediction-vs-reality format gives every post a built-in narrative arc — people share because the gap between AI expectation and real life is almost always funny or eerily accurate.

**The hook:** "My AI predicted a chaotic hellstorm day and it was... completely right."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — Calendar API + DALL-E 3 + single shake gesture detection. Hardest part is writing prompt templates that translate calendar data (event density, keywords, time blocks) into coherent mood/style descriptors for the image API.

### Idea 2: TapPrint

**Blocks used:** NFC (Tap) - Procedural Generation - Collect - Steps + Time + Weather (Data)

**Concept:** When two people tap phones together (NFC), both devices instantly generate the same unique piece of procedural art — a visual "fingerprint" of that moment, blended from both users' live context: steps walked that day, time of day, local weather, and a hash of the meeting timestamp. Both keep a copy in their gallery. Over weeks, your gallery becomes a visual timeline of every meaningful encounter — not a contacts list, but an art archive of who you've been physically near.

**Why it works:** Physical > digital + collection + your data IS the content. Requires genuine physical presence (can't fake an NFC tap from across the internet), so every piece in your gallery is an artifact of a real moment. The collection mechanic gives people a reason to carry their phone and tap intentionally. The gallery grows richer the more socially active you are.

**The hook:** "Every time I meet someone, we make a piece of art together — here's my gallery from the last month."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — iOS CoreNFC + deterministic procedural art (same inputs = same output on both phones is the key invariant). Hardest part is the NFC peer-to-peer session setup on iOS, which requires both devices to be in foreground with the session active simultaneously; Apple's MultipeerConnectivity may be a better fallback than raw NFC.
