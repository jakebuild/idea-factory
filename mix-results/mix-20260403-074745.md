### Idea 1: SunDebt

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** Rocket Money — proved people pay when an app turns a vague future penalty into one exact countdown and an at-risk screen they can act on now

**Blocks used:** GPS - Weather - Consequences

**Concept:** You tell the app your skin sensitivity once, log whether you used SPF, and it turns your current location, UV, and time outside into a brutally simple "visible damage countdown" instead of a generic UV index. The loop is open before a walk, beach day, lunch break, or outdoor workout, see how long you have before redness or tan-line risk spikes, then either reapply or get out of the sun. It is a better version of passive weather UV apps because onboarding sells the vanity fear directly: stop accidentally buying tomorrow's worse skin with today's nice weather.

**Reveal moment:** A blunt screen says, "At this UV and your current SPF, visible redness risk starts in 22 minutes."

**The hook:** It tells you the exact minute today's sun stops being pleasant and starts costing you your face.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is keeping the risk model conservative and personalized enough to feel useful without pretending to give medical certainty.

### Idea 2: PitchMirror

**Targets:** Status

**Copies:** Poised — proved professionals will pay for fast feedback that makes them sound sharper and more confident in high-stakes moments

**Blocks used:** Camera - Microphone - Share

**Concept:** You record a 20-second answer to "Tell me about yourself," "Why should we hire you?" or your sales opener, and the app scores executive presence using pace, filler words, smile, eye line, and posture. Then it gives one blunt retake instruction and lets you compare take one versus take two side by side, so the loop becomes record, fix one thing, rerun, send the better version to yourself before the real interview or pitch. It is better than general speaking-coach apps because it sells one fear: stop sounding junior on camera when the job, client, or first impression is on the line.

**Reveal moment:** Your first take lands at 4.8/10 presence, the retake jumps to 7.1/10, and the app highlights the exact moment your eye line and pace stopped sabotaging you.

**The hook:** It gives you a before-and-after confidence receipt before the interview even starts.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is constraining the scoring to a few defensible signals so it feels sharp instead of like fake charisma AI.

### Idea 3: Reflux Receipt

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Cal AI — reportedly does $3M+/mo and proved people will repeatedly scan what they are about to consume when the payoff is an instant, scary health verdict

**Blocks used:** Camera - Sleep - Object recognition

**Concept:** You scan the late-night meal, dessert, drink, or snack you are about to have and enter your rough bedtime, and the app predicts your reflux-risk window based on portion size, acidity, fat, spice, and how soon you plan to lie down. The loop is scan, see whether tonight's choice is likely to steal your sleep, swap to a lower-risk option or delay bedtime, and come back because the same late eating patterns keep repeating. It is better than generic reflux trackers because onboarding does not sell journaling or long-term analysis; it sells one immediate fear: will this choice punish me tonight?

**Reveal moment:** A blunt screen says, "If you eat this now and lie down at 11:15 PM, your highest heartburn risk window is 12:05 AM to 2:10 AM."

**The hook:** It tells you before the first bite whether this snack is about to turn into a miserable night.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is making the food-to-risk rules feel specific enough to be useful while staying honest that v1 is a conservative estimate, not a diagnosis.
