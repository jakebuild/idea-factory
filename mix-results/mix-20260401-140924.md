### Idea 1: PhotoBench

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved people will pay for a blunt attractiveness verdict and a clear "fix this first" path

**Blocks used:** Photos - Object recognition - Share

**Concept:** You upload 5-10 selfies or dating-profile photos and the app ranks them from most attractive to least, then explains exactly what is hurting the losers: tired eyes, weak crop, bad angle, low-status setting, forced smile. The core loop is simple: import photos, get your winner, swap in a better first photo, then come back whenever you need a new profile or post. It is a tighter, more actionable version of face scoring because users do not need abstract beauty truth, they need to know which photo gets them picked.

**Reveal moment:** "Best opener: Photo 4, 84/100 - Photo 1 makes you look older and less confident; lead with 4 tonight."

**The hook:** It ends the group-chat argument about which photo actually makes you look hottest.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is calibrating the ranking so the top pick feels consistent across different photo sets instead of random.

### Idea 2: Burn Window

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved appearance anxiety converts when the payoff is an immediate, uncomfortable score reveal users can try to improve

**Blocks used:** Camera - Weather - Challenge

**Concept:** You take a quick selfie, the app pulls local UV, asks one-time skin profile questions, and gives you a "Burn Window" for today: how many minutes you can stay out before you are likely to look visibly worse tonight. Then it gives one concrete fix like SPF, hat, or shade timing and lets you rescan later with a lower risk score. The loop is check before leaving, reduce the score, protect your skin, repeat whenever the weather changes.

**Reveal moment:** "Burn Window: 18 minutes - today's sun will age your face faster than you think; hat + SPF 50 extends you to 62."

**The hook:** It turns vague sunscreen guilt into a brutal countdown tied to how your face will actually look later.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is keeping the language conservative enough to avoid medical overclaim while still feeling sharp and urgent.

### Idea 3: ClearScore Speak

**Targets:** Intelligence

**Copies:** Duolingo — proved that score reveals, streaks, and tiny daily drills can monetize insecurity about sounding behind or inadequate

**Blocks used:** Microphone - Voice analysis - Streak

**Concept:** You read one short prompt aloud each day and the app scores how clear, confident, and competent you sound in spoken English. Instead of generic pronunciation lessons, it highlights the exact word where you lost clarity, gives a cleaner retry target, and tracks a daily "boardroom ready" score over time. The loop is one-minute drill, score reveal, instant retry, protect the streak.

**Reveal moment:** "ClearScore 67 - listeners would miss 3 key words in this answer; fix 'comfortable' and 'world' to jump above 75."

**The hook:** It tells ambitious non-native speakers whether they sound sharp or silently get written off.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is designing feedback that feels precise enough to be useful without building a full speech-learning platform in v1.
