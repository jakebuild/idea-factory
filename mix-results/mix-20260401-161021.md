### Idea 1: Leave With It

**Targets:** Fear of Loss

**Copies:** Alarmy — proved daily pain plus forced action creates habit retention far better than gentle reminders

**Blocks used:** NFC - WiFi - Consequences

**Concept:** You stick cheap NFC tags on your wallet, keys, badge, meds, or laptop charger, and the app arms a leave-home check when your phone disconnects from home WiFi during chosen hours. If an essential item has not been tapped that morning, it throws a loud lock-screen alert and keeps nagging until you confirm it or deliberately skip it. Different packs cover work, gym, travel, or school, so the app becomes a front-door ritual instead of a passive checklist.

**Reveal moment:** "Missing before exit: work badge and meds - tap both now or this alert keeps following you."

**The hook:** It turns forgetting your essentials into Alarmy for your front door.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is making the leave-home trigger reliable enough in the background without turning the app into an annoying false-alarm machine.

### Idea 2: Burn Window

**Targets:** Attractiveness

**Copies:** UMax — proved appearance anxiety converts when the payoff is an immediate, uncomfortable score reveal users want to improve

**Blocks used:** Camera - Weather - Challenge

**Concept:** You take a quick selfie, the app pulls local UV and weather, asks a one-time skin-profile quiz, and gives you a "Burn Window" for today: how many minutes you can stay outside before you are likely to look visibly worse tonight. Then it gives one concrete fix like SPF, hat, or shade timing and lets you rescan with a lower-risk setup. The loop is check before leaving, reduce the score, protect your face, repeat whenever the sun is brutal.

**Reveal moment:** "Burn Window: 18 minutes - hat + SPF 50 extends you to 62."

**The hook:** It turns vague sunscreen guilt into a brutal countdown tied to how your face will actually look later.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is keeping the language sharp enough to convert without drifting into medical overclaim.

### Idea 3: ClearScore Speak

**Targets:** Intelligence

**Copies:** Duolingo — proved that score reveals, streaks, and tiny daily drills can monetize insecurity about sounding behind or inadequate

**Blocks used:** Microphone - Voice synthesis - Streak

**Concept:** You read one short prompt aloud each day and the app scores how clear, confident, and competent you sound in spoken English. Instead of generic pronunciation lessons, it highlights the exact words that got muddy, plays back a cleaner target version, and gives you one instant retry to raise your score. The loop is one-minute drill, score reveal, retry, protect the streak.

**Reveal moment:** "ClearScore 67 - listeners would miss 3 key words in this answer; fix 'comfortable' and 'world' to jump above 75."

**The hook:** It tells ambitious non-native speakers whether they sound sharp or silently get written off.

**Solo ship check:** Yes + the hardest part is making the scoring feel specific enough to be trusted without building a full speech-learning platform in v1.
