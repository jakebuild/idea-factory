### Idea 1: BioAge

**Targets:** Health Anxiety / Fear of Aging

**Copies:** InsideTracker — $100M+ in revenue, proved that people will obsessively track a "biological age" number and pay to improve it, even when the score makes them feel bad about themselves

**Blocks used:** Apple Health data input - AI scoring model - score reveal with per-factor breakdown

**Concept:** Connect Apple Health, answer 5 quick questions (diet quality, alcohol, supplements, stress level), and get your Biological Age Score — how old your body actually is vs your birth certificate. InsideTracker charges $200+ for lab tests to generate the same number; BioAge uses data you already own and delivers the score in 30 seconds. Each contributing factor shows exactly how many years it is adding to or subtracting from your biological age, so the verdict is actionable, not just frightening.

**Reveal moment:** "Your biological age is 38. You're 31. These three habits are the reason."

**The hook:** "I'm 29 but apparently my body thinks it's 36 — the sleep section alone was brutal."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — Apple Health API is well-documented; the hard part is building a defensible scoring formula, but weighting published research factors (sleep debt, resting HR, VO2 max, HRV, steps) into a single score is achievable in 2–4 weeks without any lab integrations.

### Idea 2: HookScore

**Targets:** Creator Anxiety / Status (Fear of Being Ignored)

**Copies:** CoSchedule Headline Analyzer — $15M+ ARR, proved that content creators will obsessively rewrite a single line of copy to chase a score, and share it publicly when it is high

**Blocks used:** Text input - AI scoring engine - word-level rewrite with score delta

**Concept:** Paste the opening sentence of your TikTok, Reel, or YouTube script and get a Hook Score (0–100) that predicts scroll-stopping power, with the exact word or phrase that is killing retention highlighted in red. Unlike generic writing tools, HookScore is tuned specifically on viral short-form video openings and frames the score around the 3-second skip decision. One free score per day forces the habit without killing the paywall.

**Reveal moment:** "Your hook scores 28/100. You lose viewers at 'So today I wanted to talk about' — here is the rewrite that scores 84."

**The hook:** "My usual intro tested at 23. The AI rewrote it to 87 and my next video got 3x my average watch time."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — a GPT-4 API call with a carefully engineered scoring prompt handles the core loop; the challenge is making the score feel consistent and defensible, which is solvable with prompt engineering and a small calibration dataset of known-viral hooks.

### Idea 3: PairSync

**Targets:** Relationship Anxiety / Fear of Growing Apart

**Copies:** Wordle (NY Times, 300M+ plays) — proved that a once-daily shared challenge with a shareable result and no second chances drives massive word-of-mouth and daily return without any gamification overhead

**Blocks used:** Daily question mechanic - partner comparison reveal - sync score with streak

**Concept:** Every day, you and your partner each independently answer the same question about each other ("What is your partner most stressed about this week?"). After both answer, the app reveals both responses side by side and shows your Sync Score. Like Wordle there is only one question per day, and there is no correcting your answer after you see theirs — the mismatch is the point. The question bank is drawn from Gottman Institute research so it surfaces real gaps, not trivia.

**Reveal moment:** "You got 2 of 3 right. Your partner thinks you are stressed about money. You said work. They also think you need more alone time — you had no idea."

**The hook:** "We have been together 4 years and scored 1 out of 3. We spent the next hour actually talking."

**Solo ship check:** Yes — a simple back-end with real-time sync between two users is straightforward; the hardest part is writing a question bank that feels insightful rather than corny, which is a weekend of writing, not engineering.
