### Idea 1: Landing Window

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** Timeshifter — proved people will pay for a personalized jet-lag plan when the payoff is a sharp, actionable schedule instead of generic travel tips.

**Blocks used:** Sleep - Calendar - GPS

**Concept:** Forward your flight or enter the route manually, then the app turns your trip into a simple recovery plan built around bedtime, local sunlight, and your actual meeting schedule after landing. Instead of showing a big biohacker protocol, it only sells the next 1-3 actions that matter right now: when to seek light, when to stop caffeine, and when a short nap helps versus hurts. It is a better solo-dev wedge than a full travel app because the product is one tight promise: lose less of your first day.

**Reveal moment:** "Ignore the plan and you'll land 5.8 hours off; follow these next three actions and you cut it to 1.9 hours."

**The hook:** It turns vague travel fatigue into one brutal before-and-after number before you even leave for the airport.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is building a conservative circadian rules engine that feels credible without overpromising or requiring airline integrations.

### Idea 2: Panic Brake

**Targets:** Status

**Copies:** Poised — proved professionals will pay for feedback that helps them sound sharper and more confident in high-stakes conversations.

**Blocks used:** Microphone - Heart rate - Calendar

**Concept:** Two minutes before a meeting, interview, sales call, or date, the app runs a fast pre-performance check. You read one short prompt, it measures pace, filler words, volume consistency, and camera-based pulse, then gives you a 60-second reset drill and a second retest so the loop becomes check, reset, retest, walk in greener than before. It is stronger than a generic speaking coach because onboarding does not sell long-term improvement; it sells one thing: stop sounding rattled in the next important moment.

**Reveal moment:** "Your pulse dropped from 101 to 82 and your filler-word risk flipped from red to green before the meeting starts."

**The hook:** It gives you a screen-recordable before-and-after confidence receipt in under 90 seconds.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is keeping the analysis narrow and objective enough to feel trustworthy across accents and noisy rooms.

### Idea 3: Spiral Receipt

**Targets:** Health

**Copies:** How We Feel — proved people will log emotions when the payoff is feeling understood instead of clinically tracked.

**Blocks used:** Sleep - Calendar - Habits

**Concept:** You tap one mood each evening and optionally log simple tags like caffeine, alcohol, workout, conflict, or late meal. The app pulls sleep and calendar context automatically, then each week gives you one pattern card and one tiny experiment for the next seven days, such as no caffeine after 2 PM or no back-to-back evening meetings. It avoids the generic mood-tracker trap by selling diagnosis, not journaling.

**Reveal moment:** "Your last 7 anxious nights were all preceded by under 6 hours of sleep plus 2 or more evening meetings."

**The hook:** It finally gives people proof that their bad nights are not random.

**Solo ship check:** Yes — the hardest part is presenting correlations conservatively so the app feels insightful instead of fake-causal.
