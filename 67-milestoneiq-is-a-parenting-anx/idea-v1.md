# Idea #67: MilestoneIQ is a parenting anxiety app that copies the Wonder Weeks model but replaces the fixed developmental calendar with a personalized observation log that generates a unique Developmental Age score for each child. Each week, parents answer 3 quick yes/no questions about their baby or toddler — did they point at something new, say a new word, pull to stand. Takes 60 seconds. The app maps responses against CDC and WHO developmental norms and calculates a Developmental Age score showing where the child sits relative to their actual age across four domains: motor, language, social, and cognitive. The reveal is immediate and screen-recordable: a bold animated number — 'Your 14-month-old has the language development of a 17-month-old' — followed by a color-coded growth curve per domain and one specific at-home activity to close the lowest-scoring gap. The loop is log this week's observations, see the score update, share the card with a partner or grandparent, and come back next week because developmental windows are time-sensitive and missing a week feels like falling behind. Monetize with a free score reveal for the first 4 weeks and a paid tier that unlocks full historical trend tracking, sibling comparison, pediatrician-ready PDF export, and early warning alerts when a domain score drops below the 25th percentile.
