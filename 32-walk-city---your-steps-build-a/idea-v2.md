## Original Idea
Walk City - your steps build a city that grows with you - Every day your steps generate a unique cityscape - Walk 5000 steps modest town, 15000 steps towering metropolis - different times create different lighting - miss a day city sleeps - AI procedurally generates based on your data - collection grows daily - revisit at night to see where you walked
## What Changed and Why
- Replaced the vague "AI-generated cityscape" with a deterministic, persistent city builder. Each day’s steps add one explainable block to a single city, so users can see exactly why the city changed and the solo build stays technically manageable.
- Cut location history, night revisit behavior, and the "city sleeps if you miss a day" punishment. These features added privacy risk, guilt, and scope without strengthening retention, so v1 now uses only daily step count plus a forgiving streak model.
- Narrowed the product to an iPhone-first habit loop with one city, one archive, one progression mechanic, and one data source spike. This makes the product shippable in 2-4 weeks and gives users a real reason to return: persistent district growth and landmark unlocks every few active days.
## Improved Description
Walk City v2 is an iPhone-first step companion where each active day places a permanent new block into one shared city instead of generating a disposable postcard. The product is not a quantified-self dashboard and not an AI art toy. It is a lightweight habit loop: open the app, sync today’s steps, see what got built, and work toward the next district upgrade or landmark unlock.

The city is generated by a small deterministic ruleset:
- 0-3,999 steps: add a small residential block
- 4,000-7,999 steps: add a mixed-use block
- 8,000-11,999 steps: add a mid-rise block
- 12,000+ steps: add a high-rise or civic block
- Every 3 active days: unlock one permanent landmark blueprint
- Every 7 active days in a rolling window: upgrade one district skin or utility bonus such as parks, transit, or waterfront

Daily output is explainable. The app shows a short build log such as "Today added 1 mixed-use block because you walked 6,420 steps" and "Landmark unlocked: library after 3 active days." That fixes the original problem where the visuals were pretty but meaningless.

Why users come back in week 2: the city compounds. Users are no longer checking a fresh image with no memory. They are trying to complete districts, unlock the next landmark every few active days, and fill out their 14-day archive to see the city evolve over time. The medium-term reward is the persistent city map, not a one-day novelty reveal.

V1 removes the privacy and complexity traps completely:
- No GPS history
- No "see where you walked"
- No daily AI image generation
- No social features
- No user-generated districts or marketplace mechanics
- No punishment for missed days

Missed days do not decay the city. They simply pause growth. That keeps the emotional tone encouraging instead of punitive.

The product pitch is not "we have magic step-sync tech." The differentiator is the explainable city progression layer on top of step data. If direct HealthKit access proves annoying in the chosen stack, that is a delivery risk, not the moat.

3 biggest assumptions that could kill the product:
- UNVERIFIED: Read-only daily step sync from HealthKit can be implemented cleanly in the chosen iPhone stack within a 2-day spike. Fallback plan: keep sync out of the product pitch, run a short technical spike first, and if it slips, ship a closed prototype with seeded demo data to validate the city loop before doing more platform work.
- UNVERIFIED: Users will care enough about persistent city progression to return after the first week. Fallback plan: instrument D1/D7 retention and archive opens; if users only view once, stop before expanding scope.
- FALSE: Punishing missed days improves motivation. The critique strongly suggests this would cause guilt and uninstall risk, so v1 removes decay entirely.
## Why This Is Worth Building (Solo + AI)
This is worth building because it turns the original poetic concept into a tight habit product with one clear loop, one visual system, and one progression mechanic that AI coding tools can help ship quickly. A solo developer can generate the UI, deterministic rendering logic, simple unlock system, and analytics instrumentation fast, while avoiding the expensive traps of AI image generation, location privacy, and cross-platform health integrations.
## Solo MVP Scope
- What's in v1: iPhone-first app, one read-only step source attempt, deterministic city renderer, one persistent city with 4 district types, build log explaining changes, 14-day archive, landmark unlock every 3 active days, forgiving streak tracking, onboarding, and lightweight analytics.
- What's out of v1: Android, web/PWA, GPS or route history, "see where you walked," AI image generation, social sharing mechanics beyond native screenshot share, leaderboards, friends, challenges, payments, watch app, multiple cities, weather systems, manual content moderation, and any marketplace or UGC layer.
- Estimated build time with AI coding tools: 2.5 to 3.5 weeks total, including 2 days for HealthKit integration spike, 5-6 days for UI and deterministic renderer, 2-3 days for progression/archive logic, 1 day for landmark copy and metadata prep, 1-2 days for analytics/instrumentation, and 2-3 days for testing, polish, and TestFlight setup.

## Design Handoff
List every screen/view the app needs so a designer or AI design tool can start immediately:
- Screen 1: Onboarding — explain the core loop, show one mock city example, ask for Health permissions, and set expectation that the app builds one persistent city from daily steps.
- Screen 2: Permission Setup — focused screen for HealthKit access with concise rationale, success state, failure state, and fallback note if sync is unavailable during the prototype phase.
- Screen 3: Home City View — main daily screen showing the city scene, today’s step count, today’s new block, next unlock progress, and a short build log explaining exactly what changed.
- Screen 4: District Detail — zoomed-in view of one district showing blocks already placed, district level, upcoming upgrade, and landmark slots.
- Screen 5: Landmark Unlock — celebratory modal or screen showing the unlocked landmark, why it unlocked, where it appears in the city, and a share image action.
- Screen 6: Archive — 14-day timeline of prior city states with date, step count, block type added, and landmark or district upgrades.
- Screen 7: Daily Summary — compact stats screen for active days, current streak style metric, total blocks built, unlocked landmarks, and next milestone.
- Screen 8: Settings — permissions status, notification toggle, analytics/privacy copy, reset city option, and app version/support links.
- Screen 9: Empty/No Data State — shown when no steps are available yet; explains what the app needs, includes demo city preview, and routes back to permission setup.
Key interactions:
- User completes onboarding, grants permissions, lands on Home City View, and sees today’s build outcome.
- From Home City View, user taps the new block or district to open District Detail and understand progress.
- When a milestone is reached, Landmark Unlock appears and returns the user to the city with the new landmark placed.
- User can open Archive from Home City View to compare how the city changed day by day.
- If sync fails or no data exists, the app routes to Empty/No Data State and then back to Permission Setup.
