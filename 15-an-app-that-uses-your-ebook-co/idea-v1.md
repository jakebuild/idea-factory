# Idea #15: an app that uses your ebook collection to generate rich previews (summaries, mind maps, reviews) for books users scan at bookstores, helping them decide before buying, with affiliate links to purchase the real book

## Version: v1 (Original)
**Date:** 2026-03-25 22:33
**Status:** submitted

## Description
an app that uses your ebook collection to generate rich previews (summaries, mind maps, reviews) for books users scan at bookstores, helping them decide before buying, with affiliate links to purchase the real book

### Critique
**Verdict:** NEEDS MAJOR WORK | **Score:** 5/10

The bookstore scan-to-decide use case is real and the personalization angle is novel. But the idea has two near-fatal flaws: (1) using ebook text for AI generation is a copyright minefield — DRM, publisher rights, and redistribution of AI-derived content will trigger takedowns; (2) most users' ebook libraries are DRM-locked on Kindle/Apple Books and physically inaccessible to a third-party app. The monetization via affiliate links is also confused — users are standing in a store where they can just buy the book. The better pivot: ditch the ebook content angle, use reading history/taste profiles from Goodreads/StoryGraph instead, and sell a "should I buy this?" personalized score. Drop mind maps. Ship summary + taste match + recommendation. That's a 2-week MVP without the legal exposure.
