You photograph a prescription label, enter roughly how many pills are left, and the app calculates your exact run-out date, refill lead time, and risk window before you get stuck without medication. ## Description
Refill Clock is a fear-of-loss app that copies the Rocket Money model but applies it to prescription anxiety instead of subscriptions. You photograph a prescription label, enter roughly how many pills are left, and the app calculates your exact run-out date, refill lead time, and risk window before you get stuck without medication. The reveal is instant and screen-recordable: a blunt screen that says 'You will run out of Lexapro in 6 days and your refill usually needs 3 business days,' plus a countdown and escalating reminders before the cliff. The core loop is scan once, make tiny pill-count adjustments, and come back because refill panic is not a one-time problem.
