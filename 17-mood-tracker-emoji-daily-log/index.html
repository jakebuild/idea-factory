## Idea #17: Mood Tracker — Tap an Emoji to Log How You Feel A simple mobile app where you tap an emoji to log how you feel each day. One tap, done. Over time it builds a mood calendar so you can see patterns — "I'm always low on Mondays" or "I feel great after gym days." No journaling, no long forms. Just an emoji and optionally a one-line note.
🔍 Critique v1 6/10
2026-03-27 ▼
NEEDS MAJOR WORK — Market saturated, emoji alone is shallow data, no retention hook
**Verdict:** NEEDS MAJOR WORK **Score:** 6/10 **Brutal Feedback:** • This market is saturated. Daylio, Bearable, Finch, Reflectly — all do this. "Simpler" is not a differentiation strategy. • Emoji alone is shallow data. Without knowing what "😔" means, patterns are noise. • No answer to: why would someone open this every day? Habit apps live or die by their retention hook. **Suggestions:** • Pick one specific user problem: correlate mood with sleep/exercise • Add a daily push notification to drive retention • Make the pattern view the hero — lead with insight, not logging • Consider a widget so logging happens without opening the app
✨ Improved v2
2026-03-27 ▼
MoodDot — widget-first mood tracker with habit correlation patterns
**What Changed:** • Picked specific job: correlate mood with daily habits (slept well, exercised, work stress) • Made widget the primary entry point — one tap, no app launch required • Pattern view is now the hero: "On days you tag 'good sleep' your mood averages 4.1/5" • Dropped cloud sync for v1 — local-only removes auth, backend costs, privacy concerns **MVP Scope:** • In v1: Home screen widget (5 emoji), context tags (12 presets), local storage, weekly correlation summary, pattern screen (30-day view) • Out of v1: Cloud sync, custom tags, social sharing, AI insights, streak tracking, export • Estimated: 2–3 weeks with AI coding tools
